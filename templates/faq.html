Frequently Asked Questions

About OpenPrescribing Hospitals

What is OpenPrescribing Hospitals?

OpenPrescribing Hospitals is a platform that provides open access to NHS hospital medicines data, allowing healthcare professionals and researchers to explore stock control data across different hospital trusts.

Data Source

Where do you get the data from?

The OpenPrescribing Hospitals platform uses the Secondary Care Medicines Dataset (SCMD). The data held in the SCMD are collated by Rx-info and hosted on the National Health Service Business Services Authority (NHSBSA) Open Data Portal.

It contains usage and indicative cost for medications prescribed from all NHS Acute, Teaching, Specialist, Mental Health and Community Trusts in England (this does not include medications dispensed by community pharmacy but this is available separately).

Is there information about indication or patient age in the SCMD?

No, the SCMD does not contain any patient information as it relates only to pharmacy stock control data.

Data Status

What is provisional data?

45% of the data sources for SCMD are subject to backtracking. This means that historical prices or issues can be amended based on the actual invoiced price charged or stock issue less amount used. Backtracking is at its greatest in the most recent 3 months of data. Therefore, the SCMD data is initially published as provisional data, which is subject to change. Once backtracking is accounted for, it is published as finalised data.

Product Selection Types

What is a VMP (Virtual Medicinal Product)?

A VMP represents a single formulation (e.g., apixaban 5mg tablets). Different formulations of the same product will have different VMPs, but the same formulation made by different manufacturers will be grouped together. This is the right choice when searching for a specific formulation.

What is a VTM (Virtual Therapeutic Moiety)?

A VTM is a group containing all the formulations of a specific drug or combination (e.g., ivacaftor + tezacaftor + elexacaftor). The group will contain all the VMPs with the same exact ingredients. This is the right choice when searching for a specific treatment but need to group all formulations.

What is an Ingredient search?

Searching by ingredient groups all products containing the active ingredient, including combination products. This is useful when you want to find all medicines containing a particular substance, regardless of formulation or combinations.

Quantity Types

What is VMP Quantity?

Every VMP has a quantity unit associted with it. The VMP quantity is the total quantity of the VMP in this unit of measure.

What is Ingredient Quantity?

Ingredient quantity measures the amount of active ingredient in the medicine, typically in milligrams or grams. This is useful when comparing different formulations of the same medicine or when analysing total ingredient usage across various products.

Trust Selection

Which trusts are included in the data?

The platform includes data from all NHS hospital trusts in England that submit data to the NHS Business Services Authority (NHSBSA). This includes acute trusts, mental health trusts, and specialist trusts.

Data Presentation

What are percentile charts?

Percentile charts show the extent of variation at the level of individual Trusts. You can read more about why we use them in this blog post.
